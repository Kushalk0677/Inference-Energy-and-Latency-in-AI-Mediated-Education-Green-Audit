# Teacher Scoring Instructions

Instructions provided to the 10 Cambridge International secondary school teacher raters who scored responses in the primary study. Each teacher scored all 500 responses for their assigned precision configuration (FP16 or NF4) **blind** — without knowledge of which model generated the response.

---

## Your Task

You will evaluate AI-generated responses to secondary school questions. For each response, assign an integer score from **1 to 10** on each of the four dimensions below.

Please score based on the response itself. Do not consider how the question was asked — focus entirely on whether the AI's answer would be useful and appropriate for a secondary school student (ages 14–18).

---

## Scoring Rubric

### 1. Conceptual Accuracy (CA)

Is the response factually correct? Would it give the student an accurate understanding of the topic?

| Band | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 9–10 | Fully accurate. No errors or misleading claims. A student relying on this would develop a correct understanding. |
| 7–8  | Mostly accurate. Minor imprecisions that do not affect overall understanding. |
| 5–6  | Some inaccuracies. At least one concept is imprecise or partially wrong in a way a student might notice. |
| 3–4  | Multiple errors. The response is unreliable and could cause confusion. |
| 1–2  | Fundamentally incorrect. The response would actively mislead the student. |

---

### 2. Clarity & Coherence (CC)

Is the response easy to follow? Is it well-organised and clearly written?

| Band | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 9–10 | Exceptionally clear. The explanation flows logically and is easy to read throughout. |
| 7–8  | Clear, with good logical flow. Minor structural issues that do not affect comprehension. |
| 5–6  | Moderately clear. Some sections are confusing, poorly worded, or structured. |
| 3–4  | Hard to follow. The structure is disorganised or the language is frequently confusing. |
| 1–2  | Incoherent. The response cannot be understood as a coherent explanation. |

---

### 3. Scaffolding Quality (SQ)

Does the response build understanding step by step? Does it use examples, analogies, or progressive explanations to help the student learn?

| Band | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 9–10 | Excellent scaffolding. Builds understanding progressively with strong worked examples, analogies, or step-by-step reasoning. A student working through a problem would be genuinely guided. |
| 7–8  | Good scaffolding. Logical progression with some examples or steps. Knowledge builds well. |
| 5–6  | Partial scaffolding. Some structure present but insufficient examples or progression for a learner who needs guidance. |
| 3–4  | Little scaffolding. The response jumps to conclusions without building understanding. |
| 1–2  | No scaffolding. Bare assertion with no explanatory support or worked reasoning. |

**Note on SQ:** This dimension reflects your professional judgment about *authentic pedagogical progression*, not just the presence of numbered steps or bullet points. A response with numbered steps that does not build genuine understanding should receive a moderate score, not a high one.

---

### 4. Level Appropriateness (LA)

Is the response pitched at the right level for a secondary school student (ages 14–18)?

| Band | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 9–10 | Perfectly calibrated. Language, vocabulary, and depth are ideal for the target age group. |
| 7–8  | Appropriate. Minor over- or under-pitching that does not affect usefulness. |
| 5–6  | Somewhat mismatched. Either too advanced (uses unexplained technical language) or too simplistic (patronising or incomplete). |
| 3–4  | Clearly mismatched. Either well beyond the level of the student or far too simple to be instructive. |
| 1–2  | Entirely inappropriate. The response would not be useful to a secondary school learner regardless of its accuracy. |

---

## Practical Guidance

- **Score each dimension independently.** A response can be accurate but poorly scaffolded, or well-scaffolded but slightly inaccurate. Do not let your view of one dimension pull other scores.
- **Use the full scale.** Reserve 9–10 for genuinely excellent responses, and 1–2 for clearly failing ones. Most responses will fall in the 6–9 range.
- **Score blind.** You will not be told which AI model generated the response. This is intentional — please score based on the response content only.
- **No partial scores.** Scores must be integers (whole numbers only).
- **If unsure**, use the band descriptions to anchor your score. The midpoint of a band (e.g., 7 for the 7–8 band) is a safe default if the response fits the band but does not strongly lean toward either end.

---

## How to Record Scores

Enter scores in the provided spreadsheet. For each response row:

- Column `CA` → Conceptual Accuracy score (1–10)
- Column `CC` → Clarity & Coherence score (1–10)
- Column `SQ` → Scaffolding Quality score (1–10)
- Column `LA` → Level Appropriateness score (1–10)

Do not modify any other columns. Leave comments in the `Notes` column if you wish to flag something unusual.

---

## After Scoring

Once you have completed scoring, a brief structured interview (10–15 minutes) will be conducted to confirm you applied the rubric as intended and to capture any patterns or observations you noticed across responses. There are no right or wrong answers — the goal is to understand any systematic differences in how you interpreted the rubric dimensions.
